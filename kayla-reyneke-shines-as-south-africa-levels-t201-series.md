# Kayla Reyneke Shines as South Africa Levels T20I Series

South Africa Women showcased a thrilling performance to **level their T20I series** in Hamilton, with teenage all‑rounder **Kayla Reyneke** playing a pivotal role in the comeback win against **New Zealand Women**. Reyneke’s explosive finish and the team’s collective effort helped the Proteas post a competitive total and secure an 18‑run victory, marking a key moment in the series. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

The hosts posted a strong total of **177 for 5**, boosted by captain **Laura Wolvaardt’s solid 41* from 33 balls** and Tazmin Brits’ half‑century. Reyneke’s quickfire cameo of **28 not out off just nine balls**, featuring THREE consecutive sixes, swung the momentum firmly in South Africa’s favour. Her innings electrified the Hamilton crowd and gave the Proteas a target that proved too steep for the White Ferns. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

## Reyneke’s Match‑Turning Cameo

Reyneke’s cameo was the defining highlight of the match. Coming in during the latter stages of the batting innings, the 20‑year‑old blasted three sixes in as many balls off New Zealand’s **Sophie Devine**, turning a tense run chase into a manageable total and energising the South African camp. The late‑order fireworks kept the Proteas in control and ultimately helped them set a competitive target that New Zealand couldn’t reach. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

## Bowling and Fielding Efforts

While Reyneke’s batting stole the spotlight, South Africa’s bowlers kept the pressure on throughout the New Zealand innings. Seamers like **Ayabonga Khaka** and spinner **Nonkululeko Mlaba** chipped in with regular breakthroughs to restrict the White Ferns, preventing them from mounting a successful chase. Regular wickets and disciplined lines meant New Zealand always trailed the required scoring rate, allowing the Proteas to maintain control even when early breakthroughs were elusive. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

## Series Context and What’s Next

The series between South Africa and New Zealand now stands level, giving the Proteas a psychological boost heading into the next T20I fixture. Reyneke’s performance builds on her rise from the under‑19 ranks to senior international cricket — she previously captained South Africa’s U19 side and earned her maiden senior call‑up last year. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

Her match‑winning form not only illustrates her potential as one of the Proteas’ most exciting young all‑rounders but also highlights the depth in South African women’s cricket as they prepare for future international assignments. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

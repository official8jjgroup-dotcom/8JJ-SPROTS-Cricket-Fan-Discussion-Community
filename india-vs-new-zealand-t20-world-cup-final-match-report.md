# [India vs New Zealand: T20 World Cup Final Match Report](https://8jj.com/india-vs-new-zealand-t20-world-cup-final/)

**India produced a commanding performance to defeat New Zealand in the ICC Men’s T20 World Cup final**, securing a historic victory in front of a packed crowd at the Narendra Modi Stadium in Ahmedabad.

India’s batting lineup delivered a powerful display, posting a massive total of **255 for 5 in 20 overs**. The innings was anchored by an explosive knock from Sanju Samson, who continued his outstanding form throughout the tournament and played a crucial role in setting a daunting target.

New Zealand faced immediate pressure during the chase as India’s bowlers struck early. Despite some resistance from the middle order, the Black Caps struggled to maintain the required scoring rate against a disciplined Indian attack.

Fast bowler **Jasprit Bumrah** produced a decisive spell, dismantling the New Zealand lineup and ensuring the chase never gained momentum. India’s bowlers combined pace, accuracy, and aggressive field placements to keep the opposition under constant pressure.

New Zealand were eventually bowled out for **159**, handing India a comprehensive **96-run victory** in the tournament final.

The triumph marked **India’s third ICC Men’s T20 World Cup title** and their second consecutive championship, reinforcing their dominance in the shortest format of the game. The victory also sparked celebrations across the country, with fans and former players praising the team’s fearless style of cricket throughout the competition.

India’s success in the tournament was built on a balanced squad that combined explosive batting with a versatile bowling attack. From commanding powerplay performances to decisive spells in the death overs, the team consistently delivered under pressure during the tournament’s most important matches.

The final against New Zealand ultimately showcased the strength and depth of India’s lineup, confirming their status as one of the most formidable teams in world T20 cricket.

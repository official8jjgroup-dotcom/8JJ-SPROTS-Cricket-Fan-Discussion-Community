# [Salman Ali Agha Urges Sportsman Spirit After Controversial Run-Out](https://8jj.com/salman-ali-agha-urges-sportsman-spirit-after-controversial/)

Pakistan batter Salman Ali Agha has called for greater emphasis on sportsmanship in cricket after a controversial run-out incident during the second ODI between Pakistan and Bangladesh in Dhaka.

The dismissal occurred in the 39th over when Mohammad Rizwan drove a delivery back toward Bangladesh captain Mehidy Hasan Miraz. The ball deflected and rolled toward the non-striker’s end where Agha was standing outside his crease. As Agha bent down to pick up the ball, apparently intending to return it to the bowler, Miraz quickly grabbed it and broke the stumps to run him out.

Television replays confirmed that Agha was outside his crease when the stumps were broken, and the third umpire upheld the dismissal. However, the unusual nature of the incident sparked a major debate among fans and commentators about whether the act aligned with the spirit of the game.

Speaking after the match, Agha acknowledged that the run-out was within the laws of cricket but said he personally would have handled the situation differently. He explained that he believed the ball had already struck his pad and bat and therefore assumed the play was effectively over.

Agha admitted his frustration in the immediate aftermath of the dismissal but later described it as a reaction in the heat of the moment. He added that while the dismissal was technically legal, he believes sportsmanship should remain a key part of the game.

The Pakistan batter also revealed that there were some heated exchanges between players during the incident, although he said such moments are common in competitive matches and that there were no lasting issues between the teams.

Despite the controversy surrounding the dismissal, Pakistan went on to secure a comfortable victory in the match, leveling the three-match ODI series and setting up an intriguing decider.

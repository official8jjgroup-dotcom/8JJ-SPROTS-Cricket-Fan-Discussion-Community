# Sinner Streak Ends but Quarter-Final Spot Secured

**Jannik Sinner** saw his remarkable streak come to an end but still managed to secure a place in the **Monte Carlo Masters 2026** quarter-finals after a hard-fought victory over **Tomas Machac**.

## Historic Streak Comes to an End

Sinner’s run of consecutive sets without defeat at **ATP Masters 1000** events was finally broken. He had gone through multiple tournaments — including titles in Paris, Indian Wells, and Miami — without dropping a single set before this match.

The streak ended when Machac claimed the second set in a tense tiebreak, halting one of the most impressive runs in recent tennis history. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

## Hard-Fought Victory

Despite the setback, Sinner showed resilience to win the match **6-1, 6-7(3), 6-3**. After dominating the first set, he faced a dip in energy during the second but recovered strongly in the decider to close out the match. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

## Signs of Fatigue

Sinner admitted after the match that fatigue played a role in his performance. The demanding schedule and his recent dominance may have contributed to the temporary drop in intensity during the second set.

Still, his ability to push through and secure the win highlighted his growing maturity and mental strength on tour. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

## Quarter-Final Clash Ahead

With the victory, Sinner advanced to the quarter-finals, where he is set to face **Felix Auger-Aliassime**. The Canadian progressed after his opponent retired, meaning Sinner could face a fresher challenger in the next round. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

## Momentum Still Intact

Although the streak has ended, Sinner’s overall form remains exceptional. He continues to be one of the most dominant players on the ATP Tour and remains a strong contender for the title in Monte Carlo.

Sinner’s ability to win even when not at his best underlines why he is considered one of the top players in the world today. The streak may be over, but his pursuit of more titles continues.

# Kayla Reyneke Shines as South Africa Levels T20I Series

South Africa Women fought back to level their **T20I series against New Zealand** with a thrilling 18‑run victory in the second match at **Seddon Park, Hamilton**. The Proteas’ resurgence was spearheaded by explosive batting from 20‑year‑old all‑rounder **Kayla Reyneke**, who delivered a pivotal late cameo to help her team post a competitive total. 

After winning the toss, the Proteas Women made a strong start through opener **Tazmin Brits**, who top‑scored with **53 off 43 balls**, and captain **Laura Wolvaardt**, finishing unbeaten on **41 from 33 deliveries**. Their partnership laid the foundation for a competitive score of **177 for 5**, with Reyneke’s quickfire **28 not out off just nine balls**, including three consecutive sixes off New Zealand stalwart Sophie Devine, providing a decisive late‑innings boost. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

## Reyneke’s Match‑Winning Cameo

Reyneke’s cameo proved to be the turning point of the match. Arriving late in the innings, the young all‑rounder unleashed a flurry of big hits in the final overs that lifted South Africa into a commanding position. Her powerful batting not only swung the momentum in the Proteas’ favour but also earned her the **Player of the Match** award in just her third senior T20I outing. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

## Bowling and Fielding Performances

With the ball, South Africa’s bowling unit kept constant pressure on the White Ferns. **Ayabonga Khaka** led the attack with an impressive **4 for 27**, while spinner **Nonkululeko Mlaba** supported well, taking **3 for 27**. Their disciplined bowling display helped restrict New Zealand to **159 all out**, despite resistance from the hosts. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

The Proteas’ fielding was also sharp, contributing to crucial breakthroughs and maintaining pressure throughout the New Zealand innings. South Africa’s combined effort in batting, bowling, and fielding ensured the visitors secured a significant win to level the series at **1‑1**. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

## Looking Ahead

The victory sets up an intriguing remainder of the five‑match series, with South Africa and New Zealand scheduled to meet in the next T20I later this week. Reyneke’s continued rise — from captaining the South Africa Under‑19 Women’s side at the ICC U19 T20 World Cup to impactful senior performances — highlights her growing importance in the Proteas setup. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}
